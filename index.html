Phase 1: Kick-Off & Foundation (January)

The goal is to create a major initial impact and ensure everyone knows the competition has started and what's at stake.

Awareness Activations:

  • Launch Video: Create a short video (1-2 minutes) with the CEO or a director (or avatar) announcing "Q1 Ignition" and the "Trade to the Top" competition. A video creates more emotional impact.
  • Office Branding: Decorate the office with the campaign's branding. Use posters in high-traffic areas.
  • Digital Launch Kit: We will send a welcome announcement via Teams with a "kit" that includes: a link to the rules and the phase calendar.

Engagement Activations:

  • "First Strike" Prize: Offer a symbolic prize (e.g., a â‚¬20 lunch voucher) to the first person who gets the first FTD for FX Pro. This creates an immediate sense of urgency.
  • Real-Time Leaderboard: Use a tool like Power BI or even a simple shared spreadsheet to create a leaderboard that is updated daily. Visual competition is a great motivator.

Phase 2: The Q1 Skill Forge (February)

The focus is to keep the energy high but channel it into skill development. The activations should value learning.

Awareness Activations:

  • "Meet the Expert": Before the Moneyflix workshop, share quotes from the trainer to generate interest and credibility.
  • Content Teasers: Post "knowledge pills" on the Teams channel. For example: "Did you know...? Next week in our workshop, we will delve deeper into this topic!".
  • Quiz Countdown: In the days leading up to the certification quiz, run a countdown, reminding everyone of the cash prize for the top 20.

Engagement Activations:

  • Live Q&A Session: After the workshop, organize an informal Q&A session with the Moneyflix expert so people can ask specific questions.

Phase 3: Acceleration & Momentum (March)

This is the final month of the competition. The goal is to combat fatigue and create an exciting final sprint.

Awareness Activations:

  • Report: In early March, share a visual summary of the competition's progress. "We're almost at the end! See who's in the lead and who's climbing the ranks!".
  • "Success Spotlight": Highlight a team or individual who had a particularly good week. Share a short story about the strategy they used (without revealing secrets) to inspire others.
  • "Milestone" Alerts: Publicly celebrate when the entire team reaches important goals (e.g., 500 total FTDs).

Engagement Activations:

  • "Power Week": Announce a "Power Week" where FTDs or impressions count double. This can turn the game around and give a chance to those who are further behind.

Phase 4: Review & Recognition (April)

The goal is to celebrate success, recognize everyone's effort, and solidify the learnings for the future.

Awareness Activations:

  • Winners' "Teaser": Before the final event, announce the nominees for different categories (not just the overall winner), such as "FTD," "Impressions."

Engagement Activations:

  • Themed Awards Ceremony: Make the "Q1 Ignition Debrief & Awards" event a true celebration. It can have a theme (hire a mechanical bull), a photobooth.
